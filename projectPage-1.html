How might Messenger use AI to help families capture memories

The project that helped me land a Facebook Internship.

I interned at Facebook this past summer working on a 0 - 1 feature on Facebook Portal. I can’t show the work I did there because it’s confidential, instead I can show the project that helped me land that internship. It centers around how Facebook could use AI during video calls to bring families closer together.

Facebook Portal

The Problem

Nowadays, families are always looking to capture memories on their phone. But what do we do when our hands are occupied like in a video call. Screen recording is an option, but requires a lot of effort and leaves spontaneous moments uncaptured.

Moments like seeing your nephew walk for the first time, your cousin announcing her engagement, or even little moments like laughing along with your family.
HIGHLIGHT

My Solution

Video Prototype

Design Challenges

DESIGN CHALLENGE #1: PRIVACY

Facebook gets a lot of criticism on how they handle privacy. This feature would need to be designed carefully to ensure people feel safe using it. One solution was to allow users to turn the feature on for specific people.

DESIGN CHALLENGE #2: IMPERFECT AI

AI isn’t perfect and needs some help from the user to avoid making the wrong decisions. One solution was to allow users to tweak memories selected for them by the AI after their call.

Process

Earlier iterations saw a version of this feature that asked users to tap on interactive emojis that the AI would send to help confirm its decisions. The thinking was that the user would have the final say on what memories were captured.

Alternate Idea

However, after doing user testing, I realized this interaction was distracting and took away from the candidness of the call. Instead, I created a post call screen that allowed users to dive deeper in to the memories the AI selected and gave them the ability to tweak them.

Conclusion

After doing this project, I decided to take it to designers working in tech hubs like San Francisco and LA at places l admired like Reddit, IDEO, and Google. Their feedback helped me refine this project and eventually I used it during my interview for Facebook and landed an internship there.
